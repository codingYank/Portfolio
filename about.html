My coding career started in my junior year of high school when I took a coding class where we recreated the legendary game “Asteroids”. I was partnered with a friend, and we called our game “Meateorites”. Instead of a spaceship shooting at rocks, we used a breadstick that fired penne noodles at meatballs (The navigation on the home page is actually inspired by that project). I had such a blast creating this game, I decided to study computer science and make a career out of coding. I was led to web development because it allows me to utilize both my analytical problem-solving and artistic and creative sides. Away from the keyboard, I am a big soccer fan as well as a sort of home chef. My love of soccer drove me to develop ftcsoccer.com, and my love of cooking pushed me to create tylers-recipes.com, a site that houses my recipes so I can share them with friends and family.
